### Results and Comparison

The Canon PIXMA G3770 had a total cost of ownership of S$4386.92 while the Brother HL-L2460DW had a total cost of ownership of S$13,307.16 over five years.

Although the Brother printer had a lower purchase price, its toner replacements significantly increased the overall ownership cost. In comparison, the Canon ink tank printer had much lower consumable costs, making it the more cost-effective option for high-volume printing.

### Reflection questions 
Based on the TCO calculation, the Canon PIXMA G3770 was the more cost-effective printer over five years because its ink costs were much lower, even though it had a higher purchase price. This showed me that buying the cheaper printer does not always mean spending less in the long run. If the printer was only used at home to print around five pages per week, i think the Brother printer could be a better option since the toner would last much longer and the lower purchase price would matter more. Besides cost, i would also consider factors such as print quality, printing speed, reliability, and how easy it is to replace ink or toner. For a large workplace, consumable costs, maintenance and reliability would become much more important because the printer is used more frequently. Overall, this lab helped me understand why it is important to look at both the initial purchase price and the long-term operating costs before deciding which printer is the better choice.
